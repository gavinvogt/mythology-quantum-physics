Introduction

Quantum physics is a mind-boggling field that seems to defy reality. In fact, it defines reality. The physics of everyday life is known as classical physics and only applies to objects that are large (compared to an atom).

When quantum mechanics is applied to a large object, its conclusions match with the classical understanding. However, classical mechanics tends to break down as objects become very small. This demonstrates that quantum physics is the more complete description of reality since it extends to a wider array of objects.

Two of the most fundamental tenets of quantum physics are quantization and superposition.
Quantization: Quantities are limited to discrete (non-continuous) values.
Superposition: A particle's state is an additive combination of every possible state that may be observed if it were measured. It is not in a well-defined state until measured!
